The Genius InPlay and PreMatch products share the same user interface and that some UI elements which are
specific to InPlay are also displayed for PreMatch. These InPlay specific elements will be removed from the

Accessing Betgenius Trading Console
Both the UAT and Production environments of the Betgenius Trading Console are accessed via web
browser. We recommend using the latest version of Chrome, but the Console is compatible with most
internet browsers.
The URLs for both UAT and Production are
• UAT: https://dataservices.uat.betgenius.com
• Production: https://dataservices.betgenius.com
Access to the In-Play and Pre-Match products is dependent on licensing but access to both products is
possible via the URLs listed above.
Logging into Betgenius Trading Console
You should have been provided with login credentials by the system
administrators within your organisation. If you need your login
credentials to be confirmed, please contact the system
administrator within your organisation.
After logging in for the first time you should navigate to the
Preferences and set your preference for the format of odds you will
view within the Betgenius Trading Console. In the top right hand
corner of the screen click into Preferences, click [change] in the row
for Odds Format, confirm your preference and click Save.
Note that the Betgenius Trading Console will not be integrated with Single Sign On (SSO) or any similar form of
federated user access tool your organisation may use.

Lists
Lists allows you to define the exact list of prices that are used to complete your desired overround and
eventually end up being sent to you from Genius.
All odds generated by PreMatch are rounded to odds in pre-determined lists known as ladders.
For each market type with more than two selections, you must supply an “Odds Ladder” consisting of all
the valid odds that you wish to be able to offer for this market type. The probabilities calculated by
Genius are first adjusted using the margin settings supplied (over-round, APM or a combination of the
two, as described above) and then rounded to the next highest or next lowest price in the chosen Odds
Ladder. Usually each price is rounded to the nearest price in the ladder, although if you have chosen to
price with an over-round then the system will also try to stay as close as possible to your chosen over-
round.
For markets with only two selections, margins and rounding are done together using a “Pairs Ladder”
consisting of all valid pairs of odds that you wish to be able to offer for this market type. Each pair in the
ladder must contain a price for the favourite and a price for the underdog, and odds will always be paired
according to the pairs within the ladder. Note that in this case there is no need to specify an overround or
APM, since the margins are implicit in the way you choose to pair the odds.

It should be noted that Odds and Pairs Ladders can contain “n/o” values (also known as “0/1” or “0” in
fractional format or as “1” in decimal format). This value can be used to represents a price of 0/1 or 1.00,
including it ensures that market containing a selection whose probability is so great that even the lowest
price in the list would be too generous, is closed.
Odds Ladders and Pairs Lists can be exported from Event Management and imported into PreMatch.
Odds Ladders
Here you will see a list of odds ladders configured for your account. You can find the name of the odds
ladder, the time it was last modified and the user who added the ladder.
If you select a ladder, you can then view the contents. The Ladders seen here are available for selection in
the Competition Templates.
Pairs Ladders
Pairs Ladders are specific to head-to-head markets such as match bets and points spread. The
functionality works the same as the odds ladders.

Because most of the settings in the Betgenius Trading Console are not user-specific, the user account
has only a few personal display settings.
To create a new user with the same personal settings as another user, use the Clone link next to the user
whose settings you wish to copy.

From the Message Board users can see all relevant updates and information pertaining to the Events

automatically, however Betgenius Trading and Support teams can also manually provide ad-hoc updates
as and when needed.

Match Trading Interface
This is the main interface used to view Markets and fixture level setting for an Event, with each Event
represented by an individual tab in your browser. At the top of the Match Trading Interface, you will find a
black ribbon which will show you:
• Name of the sport
• Name of the event showing the teams/competitors
• Scheduled start time of the Event.
While open, the Match Trading Interface maintains continuous contact with the Betgenius and provides
live updates on an Event and its Markets. Should this contact be lost for any reason (for example if your
Internet connection fails), a warning bar will be shown.
Layout of the Match Trading Interface
Within this view for Pre-Match there are three tabs; ‘Active', ’Unresulted', ‘Resulted’, ‘Settings’ and
‘Diagnostics’.
‘Active’
Displays individual Market Widgets for all markets being actively traded for the event.
‘Unresulted’
Displays individual Market Widgets for each of the markets which have been created for the event and
are not yet resulted. This tab will display all unresulted markets, regardless of their current trading status.
‘Resulted’
Displays individual Market Widgets for each of the markets which have been created for the event and
have been resulted.

Diagnostics

Market Widgets
Each Market Widget represents an individual market which has been created for the Event. Each widget
will contain
• Buttons to expand (‘+’) or collapse (‘–’) the widget.
• A market status indicator, which will either be green (trading), orange (suspended) or red
(closed). Hovering over the market status indicator will result in a additional window being
displayed which contains contextual information regarding the market.
• Name of the Market.
• All available selections for the Market with odds/prices for each. Hovering over the odds will
display the win % for the selection and the true odds/price (i.e., without margin).
Often markets will be suspended if the pricing feed is not available, or the system has identified issues
with the quality of the prices in the feed. The status of the event can also cause the markets to be
suspended i.e., the event is set to be rescheduled or cancelled.
When a Market Widget is green this indicates that the market is currently active and is receiving pricing
updates as expected.
When a Market Widget is red, this can be for two reasons.

Variation in Pricing Methods
Depending on the Sport, Pre-Match will price markets either by Consensus Pricing or Modelled
Pricing.
Regardless of the method of pricing, by default PreMatch will always attempt to create markets as soon
as it is able to calculate price.
How early it can calculate prices is dependent on several factors including the sport, tier of competition
and how popular a market is with other odds providers.
Consensus Pricing
The Betgenius Consensus Pricing algorithm takes source prices from industry pricing feeds curated by
Betgenius to provide the best possible pricing. We are able source and aggregate large amounts of
market information and then use that to calculate our own Genius price.
We do this by calculating the true probabilities from implied probabilities using our own margin stripping
algorithms and then applying them to individual selections. This calculation includes rigorous sanity
checks to always make sure that the prices our customers receive are accurate.
Unlike Event Management, PreMatch does not contain a feature for customers to create their own
custom bookmaker lists.
Modelled Pricing
Our PreMatch Football pricing works very similar to InPlay Football. We enter starting parameters and
information about the fixture to drive our models which then will return probabilities for all our market
prices.
These parameters are driven by an automated feed, which have significant sanity checking and can be
manually taken over by our Traders, to generate accurate probabilities and prices.
Each sport can differ as to the number of parameters needed depending of the market offering available.

Competition Templates
Within this view there are three options to help you control your templates; Make Default, Clone and

• Clone: An existing template can be cloned and then saved under a different name and then

• Delete: Templates can be deleted at any time.
Editing An Existing Template
To edit an existing temple, select the template name. In the top right-hand corner, you will have the same
three buttons available as described above. By clicking on the template name, you can edit and save a

Note that settings changes made at the Competition Template level will not be applied to existing
fixtures until the next pricing update for the fixture is received. Settings changes made at the Fixture
level however will be applied to the Fixture immediately.

Creating A New Template
To create a new template, click on New Blank Template and a new template will be added to the list.
Select the template name to edit the newly created template.
Event Creation
‘Create Event’ and ‘Create Markets’ are both InPlay specific features and not available for PreMatch.
Trading Active
The default setting for this field is ‘As soon as possible’, but in can be configured with other values
ranging from 90 days to 1 minute before scheduled start time.
Note that this setting does not control when the market is created, it will however dictate when a market
is active and trading. Market creation will occur as soon as PreMatch is able to price the market,
regardless of the ‘Trading Active’ setting. Where a market is created before the ‘Trading Active’ time has
passed, the market will be created with an initial state of closed.
The ‘Trading Active’ value in a Competition template will be inherited by all Fixture level templates within
the competition, however this value can be overridden from the Fixture level template.
Similarly, all individual markets within a fixture will inherit the ‘Trading Active’ value from the Competition
template, and this can be overridden at the individual market level.
Unlike the In-Play product, Pre-Match Events do not need to be booked. Markets will be created as soon
a price is available (from consensus or the probability model depending on the sport)

Overround/Adjusted Percentage Margin (APM)
The over-round on a market is a measure of the difference between the real probabilities and the
probabilities implied by the odds, calculated for each selection and then added together to give the over-
round for the whole market.

The APM is Betgenius’s system for controlling margin at the selection level rather than the market level.
It is especially useful for markets that can have a variable number of winners (for which an over-round
based on the whole market makes no sense).
For most markets with more than two possible outcomes, there are three basic options for applying
margin:
• Price using an over-round – The over-round requested will be adhered to even where one or
more probabilities are close to 0% or 100%.
• Price using an APM – The specified margin will be applied to each selection and tapered down as
the probability of a selection approaches 0% or 100%. The maximum over-round will therefore be
the APM multiplied by the number of selections (plus 100% for a one-winner market, 200% for a
two-winner market etc.).
• Price using an over-round with Maximum APM – The market will be priced using the overround or
APM specified, whichever method gives the more generous (bigger) prices. This is useful if you
want to price the market with an over-round except where one or more probabilities are close to
0% or 100%. For example, to price the Football Match Result market type you could set an over-
round of 112% with a maximum APM of 6%. This would apply the over- round under most
circumstances but would ensure that in extreme cases no selection was given an APM of more
than 6%.
